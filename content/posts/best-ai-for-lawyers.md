AI can save lawyers hours on drafting, research and review — but legal work has zero tolerance for fabricated facts or breached confidentiality. Here's how to use it safely, and which tools the profession actually relies on.

## The legal AI stack

| Task | Tools | Caution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Legal research | Harvey, CoCounsel, Lexis+ AI | Verify every citation |
| Contract review/drafting | Spellbook, CoCounsel | Human review required |
| Drafting & summarizing | ChatGPT / Claude | Check accuracy |
| Admin & email | ChatGPT / Claude | No confidential data |

## Research and contract review

For research and contract analysis, **legal-specific platforms** are far safer than general chatbots because they're grounded in verified case law. **Harvey** is built for firm-grade research and drafting; **CoCounsel** (Thomson Reuters, formerly Casetext) handles research, review and deposition prep; **Spellbook** drafts and reviews contracts inside Microsoft Word; **Lexis+ AI** ties into the Lexis database. They reduce — but don't eliminate — fabricated citations.

## Drafting and summarizing

General assistants like **ChatGPT or Claude** speed up first drafts, summaries and plain-language explanations of dense material. Claude's long context helps with lengthy documents — but keep client specifics out unless the tool meets your confidentiality requirements.

## The two rules you cannot break

> **Verify every citation.** General AI has invented fake cases — and lawyers have been sanctioned for filing them. Treat every AI-provided citation as unverified until you confirm it in a real database.
>
> **Protect confidentiality.** Never paste confidential client information into tools without proper confidentiality safeguards and your firm's approval. A convenience that breaches privilege isn't a convenience — it's a liability.

These aren't optional best practices; in legal work they're the difference between a useful tool and a career risk.

## How to start

1. Use **legal-specific AI** (Harvey, CoCounsel, Spellbook, Lexis+ AI) for research and contract work.
2. Use **ChatGPT/Claude** for drafting and summaries (no confidential data).
3. **Verify everything** and follow your jurisdiction's and firm's rules.
