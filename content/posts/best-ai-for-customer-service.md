AI can take a huge load off customer service — but only if you use it for the right parts. Here's what works, grouped by job, plus where to keep a human.

## The stack by job

| Job | Best AI | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Deflect FAQs | AI chatbot / help-desk AI | Handles common questions 24/7 |
| Draft & summarize replies | ChatGPT / Claude | Speeds up agents |
| Triage & route tickets | Help-desk AI (Intercom, Zendesk) | Sorts and prioritizes |
| Complex / sensitive cases | A human | Don't automate this |

## Deflect common questions

An AI chatbot trained on your FAQ and docs can answer repetitive questions instantly, freeing your team for harder cases. The AI features in help desks like **Intercom** and **Zendesk** are built for this.

## Draft and summarize with a general assistant

For agents, **ChatGPT or Claude** speed up the work: draft a reply, summarize a long thread, translate a message, soften a tone. Cheap, immediate wins — start here if you're small.

## Triage and route

Help-desk AI can **categorize and prioritize** incoming tickets so the urgent ones surface first.

> **The line you must not cross:** automating the cases that need a human. AI is excellent at *volume* — FAQs, drafts, routing — and bad at *nuance* — an upset high-value customer, a sensitive complaint, an edge case. Businesses that bolt AI onto everything generate angry customers and bad press. Use AI to clear the easy 70% so your people have time for the 30% that actually needs them, and always give customers an easy path to a human.

## How to start (small business)

1. Use **ChatGPT/Claude** to draft and summarize replies (free to start).
2. Add an **AI chatbot** for FAQ deflection.
3. Invest in a **help-desk AI** only when ticket volume justifies it.
