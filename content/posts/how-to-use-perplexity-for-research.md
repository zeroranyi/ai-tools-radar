Perplexity isn't just a chatbot — it's a research engine that shows its sources. Used well, it can cut hours off finding and verifying information. Here's how to get the most from it.

## Step 1: Ask specific questions

Vague questions get vague answers. Instead of "tell me about electric cars," ask "what are the main downsides of electric cars in cold climates?" Specificity gets you sharper answers and better sources.

## Step 2: Treat the citations as the feature

Every Perplexity answer links its sources. **Open them.** The real value isn't the summary — it's that you can verify each claim and read deeper. This is what separates it from a normal chatbot.

## Step 3: Use Focus modes

Narrow where it searches (e.g. academic sources vs the general web) so the answer draws from the most relevant material for your task.

## Step 4: Go deeper with follow-ups

Ask follow-up questions in the same thread. Perplexity keeps context, so you can drill from a broad overview down to specifics without starting over.

## Step 5: Organize with Spaces

For ongoing projects, group related research so you can return to it and build on previous threads.

Copying the summary and moving on without opening a single source. The summary can still contain errors — the *citations* are your safety net and your path to depth. If you only read the answer, you've turned a research engine back into a guessable chatbot.

## Free vs Pro for research

The free version is genuinely capable for everyday research. Upgrade to Pro if you research often, want deeper runs, or need to upload and query your own documents.

## Bottom line

Use Perplexity by asking precise questions and *always checking the sources it cites*. Done right, it's the fastest way to research with verification built in.
