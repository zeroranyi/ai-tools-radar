A RAG chatbot answers questions from **your own documents** — perfect for support, internal knowledge, or "chat with my docs." You can build one without writing code. Here's how.

## What RAG means (quickly)

**RAG (retrieval-augmented generation)** means the bot **retrieves** relevant passages from your documents, then **generates** an answer based on them — with sources. So it answers from *your* data, not generic training knowledge.

## Step-by-step (no code)

1. **Pick a no-code platform** that supports document upload and Q&A (several AI assistant builders do).
2. **Add your documents** — PDFs, docs, web pages, FAQs.
3. **Let it create embeddings** — the platform converts your text into searchable vectors automatically.
4. **Connect a model** — usually built in; pick the most capable available.
5. **Test with real questions** your users actually ask.
6. **Refine** — fix gaps by improving the source documents.

## Tips for a good RAG bot

- **Clean documents in, good answers out** — messy source docs = messy answers
- **Keep content current** — update documents, not the model
- **Ask it to cite sources** so users can verify
- **Test edge cases** — questions your docs don't cover

Dumping in disorganized or outdated documents and expecting magic. RAG only answers as well as the content you feed it. Spend your effort on *curating clean, current source documents* — that matters far more than which platform you pick. Garbage in, confidently-wrong out.

## Bottom line

Building a no-code RAG chatbot is genuinely achievable: choose a platform, upload clean documents, let it handle embeddings, and connect a model. The secret isn't the tech — it's the quality and freshness of the documents you give it.
