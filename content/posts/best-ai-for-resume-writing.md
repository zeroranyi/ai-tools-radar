A resume tailored to each job beats a generic one every time — and that tailoring is exactly what AI makes fast. Here's how to use it well (and the mistakes that get resumes binned).

## The resume stack

| Task | Best AI | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tailor to a job | ChatGPT / Claude | Mirror the job description |
| Cover letters | ChatGPT / Claude | Fast, then personalize |
| ATS-friendly format | AI resume builders | Templates & structure |
| Natural wording | Claude | Avoids robotic phrasing |

## Tailor to each job (the highest-leverage move)

Paste the **job description** and your resume, and ask AI to **tailor** your experience to match — mirroring the language and highlighting relevant achievements. This is far more effective than one generic resume for every application.

## Beat the ATS (truthfully)

AI can help you **mirror keywords** and structure content so applicant tracking systems parse it well. **But keep it honest** — keyword-stuffing or invented skills fall apart in interviews.

## Cover letters

Draft a tailored cover letter in seconds, then **add specifics** only you know — why this company, a real story, a concrete result.

> **The mistake that gets resumes rejected:** submitting obviously AI-written, generic text. Recruiters read hundreds of these and spot the bland, templated tone instantly. The winning approach is AI for the *heavy lifting* — tailoring, structure, first drafts — followed by **your own edit** that injects real, specific achievements in your voice. AI gets you 80% there fast; the human 20% is what gets the interview. And never let AI invent experience you don't have.

## How to use it

1. **Tailor** your resume to each job with ChatGPT/Claude.
2. **Format** with an AI resume builder for ATS-friendly layout.
3. **Rewrite in your voice** with real, specific achievements.

Most tools have free tiers — start there. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
