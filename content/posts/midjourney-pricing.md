Midjourney's pricing confuses newcomers because it's about **generation time**, not a flat "unlimited" fee. Here's how the tiers work. *Confirm current prices on Midjourney's official site.*

## How the tiers work

As of 2026, Midjourney's monthly plans are roughly:

| Tier | Price (monthly) | Roughly for | Key difference |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Basic** | ~$10 | Light/occasional | ~3.3 fast hours/mo, no relax |
| **Standard** | ~$30 | Regular creators | ~15 fast hours + unlimited relax |
| **Pro** | ~$60 | Heavy users | ~30 fast hours, stealth, more concurrency |
| **Mega** | ~$120 | Power/teams | ~60 fast hours, maximum allowances |

Annual billing knocks roughly 20% off. The main thing you buy as you go up is **more fast generation time** plus features like **relax mode** and **stealth**. *(Prices as of 2026 — confirm on Midjourney's site, as they change.)*

## Fast vs relax — the key concept

- **Fast hours** = images generate immediately, but are **limited** per plan.
- **Relax mode** (higher tiers) = **unlimited** generations at a slower, queued speed.

If you make lots of images, relax mode on a higher tier matters more than raw fast hours.

## No real free tier

Midjourney generally has **no permanent free plan** (trials come and go). If free is a must, **Stable Diffusion or Flux** are the reliable free routes.

## How to actually pick

Estimate how many images you generate a month. If you only dabble, **Basic (~$10)** is plenty. The moment you find yourself rationing generations, jump to **Standard (~$30)** — its unlimited relax mode is the real value unlock, since you stop burning limited fast hours on experiments. Pro and Mega only pay off if you generate heavily or need stealth and concurrency. And if images are going into anything commercial, confirm the license terms for your tier before you publish.

## Bottom line

Midjourney runs ~$10 / ~$30 / ~$60 / ~$120 a month (Basic → Mega), billed by generation time rather than a flat unlimited fee, with ~20% off annually and no permanent free plan.
