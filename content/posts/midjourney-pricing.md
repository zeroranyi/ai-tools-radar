Midjourney's pricing confuses newcomers because it's about **generation time**, not a flat "unlimited" fee. Here's how the tiers work. *Confirm current prices on Midjourney's official site.*

## How the tiers work

| Tier | Roughly for | Key difference |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Basic** | Light/occasional | Limited fast generation time |
| **Standard** | Regular creators | More fast time + relax (unlimited slow) |
| **Pro** | Heavy users | More fast time, stealth, concurrency |
| **Higher** | Power/teams | Maximum allowances |

The main thing you're buying as you go up is **more generation time** and features like **relax mode**.

## Fast vs relax — the key concept

- **Fast hours** = images generate immediately, but are **limited** per plan.
- **Relax mode** (higher tiers) = **unlimited** generations at a slower, queued speed.

If you make lots of images, relax mode on a higher tier matters more than raw fast hours.

## No real free tier

Midjourney generally has **no permanent free plan** (trials come and go). If free is a must, **Stable Diffusion or Flux** are the reliable free routes.

> **How to actually pick:** estimate how many images you generate a month. Light/occasional users are fine on the cheapest tier. If you generate constantly, jump to a tier with **relax (unlimited slow)** — it's cheaper than repeatedly running out of fast hours. And if you'll use images commercially, confirm the license terms for your tier before publishing.

## Bottom line

Midjourney is **tiered by generation time**, with no permanent free plan. Match the tier to your monthly volume, prioritize relax mode if you generate a lot, and check commercial terms. Confirm current pricing on Midjourney's official site.
