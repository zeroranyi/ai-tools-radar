You can run capable AI models on your own machine — free, private and offline. Here are the best open models and which fits your hardware.

## The best free local models

| Model | Known for | Hardware |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Llama** | Strong all-rounder | Scales by size |
| **DeepSeek** | Great value, coding | Scales by size |
| **Mistral** | Efficient, capable | Modest-friendly |
| **Qwen** | Strong multilingual | Scales by size |
| **Gemma** | Lightweight, efficient | Laptop-friendly |

All are **free, open-weight** models you can download and run.

## Matching model to hardware

- **8-16GB RAM (laptops):** smaller models (e.g. smaller Llama, Mistral, Gemma)
- **16-32GB + GPU:** medium models with better quality
- **32GB+ and strong GPU:** larger models approaching cloud quality

## How to run them (free tools)

- **Ollama** — one-command setup, great for developers
- **LM Studio** — graphical app, great for non-coders

Both let you download and run these models locally in minutes.

Start with a *small* model. Beginners grab the biggest one, watch it crawl or crash, and conclude "local LLMs aren't ready." A well-chosen small model runs smoothly on a normal laptop and handles most everyday tasks. Confirm your workflow on something fast, then size up only if you genuinely need more quality and your hardware can take it. Smooth and small beats sluggish and large.

## Bottom line

The best free local LLMs — Llama, DeepSeek, Mistral, Qwen, Gemma — give you private, no-cost AI on your own machine. Match the model size to your hardware, start small, and use Ollama or LM Studio to run them easily.
