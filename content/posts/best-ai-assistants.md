An AI assistant is the tool you reach for to *get work done* — drafting, analyzing, summarizing, and increasingly taking actions across your apps. Here are the best, and how to pick the one that fits your workflow.

## The best AI assistants

| Assistant | Best for | Lives in |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **ChatGPT** | All-round work | Standalone + apps |
| **Claude** | Writing & analysis | Standalone |
| **Gemini** | Google users | Gmail, Docs, Sheets |
| **Microsoft Copilot** | Microsoft 365 users | Word, Excel, Outlook, Windows |

## Pick the assistant that lives where you work

The biggest factor isn't raw capability — it's **integration**. If you live in Google Workspace, [Gemini](/posts/gemini-vs-chatgpt) saves constant copy-pasting; if you're in Microsoft 365, Copilot does the same; if your work is varied or standalone, [ChatGPT](/posts/is-chatgpt-plus-worth-it) or [Claude](/posts/is-claude-pro-worth-it) is the flexible choice.

## Assistant vs chatbot vs agent

These blur together. A [chatbot](/posts/best-ai-chatbots) emphasizes conversation; an assistant emphasizes getting work done; an [AI agent](/posts/what-is-an-ai-agent) takes multi-step actions toward a goal. The same leading tools increasingly do all three — so choose by where it fits your day, not the label.

## When to add more

Start with one general assistant. Add a specialist — a [coding tool](/posts/best-ai-coding-assistants), a [note-taker](/posts/best-ai-note-taking-app), or an [automation platform](/posts/zapier-ai-vs-make) — only when a specific, frequent need appears.

## Bottom line

ChatGPT for all-round work, Claude for writing and analysis, Gemini for Google, Copilot for Microsoft. The best assistant is the capable one that lives inside the tools you already use. Most have free tiers — browse the full landscape in the [AI Tools Directory](/tools).
