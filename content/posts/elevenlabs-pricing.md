ElevenLabs prices by **characters** (how much text you turn into speech), which trips up first-timers. Here's how the tiers work and how to pick. *Confirm current numbers on ElevenLabs' official pricing page.*

## How the pricing model works

Unlike a flat "unlimited" subscription, ElevenLabs gives you a **monthly character allowance**. More text-to-speech = a higher tier. So the first step is estimating your monthly audio volume.

| Tier | Roughly for | Key limits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Free** | Testing | Small character allowance |
| **Starter/Creator** | Solo creators | More characters, cloning, commercial use |
| **Pro / higher** | Heavy creators | Large allowances, more cloning |
| **Business/Enterprise** | Teams | High volume, admin, support |

## Estimate before you buy

A rough guide: a few thousand characters ≈ a short narration; a long video or podcast script runs into tens of thousands. **Match the tier to your monthly minutes of audio**, not to the feature list.

## What higher tiers unlock

- More **characters** per month
- More **voice cloning** slots and quality
- **Commercial rights** (essential if you monetize)
- Priority and team features at the top

Running out of characters mid-project. Because billing is volume-based, under-estimating your monthly audio means hitting a wall or paying overage. Track how many characters a typical project uses for a month, *then* pick a tier with headroom. And if you monetize, confirm your plan includes commercial rights — the cheapest tiers may not.

## Bottom line

ElevenLabs is **character-based**: free to test, paid tiers scaling by volume, cloning and commercial rights. Estimate your monthly audio needs first, then pick a tier with room to grow. Confirm current pricing on ElevenLabs' official site.
