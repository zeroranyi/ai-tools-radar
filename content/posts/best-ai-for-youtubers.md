AI can cut a YouTuber's production time dramatically — but only if you pick tools for your actual bottleneck. Here's a creator stack grouped by the stage it helps.

## The creator stack by stage

| Stage | Best AI tool | Free tier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scripting & ideas | ChatGPT / Claude | Yes |
| Voiceover | ElevenLabs | Limited |
| Editing | Descript / CapCut | Yes |
| Shorts from long video | Opus Clip | Limited |
| Thumbnails | AI image generators | Varies |

## Scripting: ChatGPT or Claude

Use a general assistant to **brainstorm titles, outline videos and draft scripts**. The trick: add your own voice and expertise so it doesn't sound like generic AI filler — that's what keeps viewers (and the algorithm) happy.

## Voiceover: ElevenLabs

For faceless channels or narration, ElevenLabs produces realistic voiceovers without recording. Clone your own voice (with consent) for consistency.

## Editing: Descript or CapCut

Descript lets you **edit video by editing text** and cleans up filler words automatically. CapCut offers AI captions, cleanup and effects. Both slash editing time.

## Repurposing: Opus Clip

Turn one long video into a batch of **short clips** for Shorts, Reels and TikTok automatically — the highest-leverage way to multiply your output.

> **The creator trap:** subscribing to every shiny AI tool and spending more time managing software than making videos. The disciplined move is to identify your single biggest bottleneck — usually editing or repurposing — and add *one* tool for it. A general assistant plus your editor covers most creators; everything else is situational.

## How to build your stack

1. Start with **ChatGPT/Claude** (scripting) + your **editor**.
2. Add **ElevenLabs** if you need voiceovers.
3. Add **Opus Clip** if shorts are your growth lever.

Most tools here have free tiers — build lean and upgrade the bottleneck. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
