Notion AI and Obsidian represent two philosophies of note-taking: a polished cloud workspace vs a private, local-first toolkit. Here's how to choose.

## The core difference

- **Notion AI** — **built-in AI** in a structured, all-in-one cloud workspace. Convenient and collaborative.
- **Obsidian** — **local-first**, private Markdown notes you own, extended by **plugins** (including AI ones).

## Side-by-side

| | Notion AI | Obsidian |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AI | Built-in, polished | Via plugins (setup) |
| Storage | Cloud | Local files you own |
| Privacy | Cloud-based | Local by default |
| Structure | Databases, all-in-one | Linked Markdown notes |
| Collaboration | Strong | Limited |
| Customization | Moderate | Deep (plugins) |

## Choose Notion AI if...

- You want **AI that just works** with no setup
- You like **structured databases** and an all-in-one workspace
- You **collaborate** with a team

## Choose Obsidian if...

- You want **privacy** and to **own your files**
- You love **customization** via plugins
- You prefer a **local-first**, future-proof Markdown vault

Convenience vs ownership. Notion is the easier, more collaborative experience with AI baked in — but your notes live in someone else's cloud. Obsidian gives you a private vault of plain files that will outlive any company, plus AI if you set it up — at the cost of doing more yourself. Knowledge workers who value permanence and privacy lean Obsidian; teams who want frictionless AI lean Notion.

## How to choose

1. **Want easy, built-in AI + collaboration?** Notion AI.
2. **Want privacy, ownership and customization?** Obsidian.
