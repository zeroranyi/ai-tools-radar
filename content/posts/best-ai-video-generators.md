AI video tools split into two jobs — *generating* footage from a prompt, and *editing* footage you already have. Here are the best for each, ranked by use case.

## Quick picks

| Goal | Best tool |
| --- | --- |
| Full creative workflow | Runway |
| Most realistic text-to-video | Sora / Veo |
| Fast, creative shorts | Pika / Kling |
| Cinematic generations | Luma |
| Edit real footage | Descript / CapCut |

## Generators (text-to-video)

- **Runway** — the most complete: generation plus editing, control and a creative suite. Best for filmmakers and marketers. See our [Runway free trial guide](/posts/runway-free-trial).
- **Sora & Veo** — the realism leaders for prompt-to-clip. Compared in detail in [Sora vs Runway vs Veo](/posts/sora-vs-runway-vs-veo).
- **Pika & Kling** — fast, creative shorts; great for social and experiments.
- **Luma** — strong cinematic generations.

## Editing real footage

If you're working with real video, you want an editor, not a generator: **Descript** edits by transcript, **CapCut** does captions and social effects ([Descript vs CapCut](/posts/descript-vs-capcut)).

## How to choose without wasting credits

AI video generation burns credits far faster than text or images, so don't treat free tiers as production tools — use them to test. Decide first whether you're *generating* or *editing*, then pick one tool for that job and learn it well. Browse more in the [AI Tools Directory](/tools).

## Bottom line

Runway for an all-in-one creative workflow, Sora or Veo for realism, Pika or Kling for fast shorts, and Descript or CapCut for editing real footage. Test on free credits first, since generation is credit-hungry. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
