Perplexity and ChatGPT get pitted against each other, but they're really built for two different jobs: **finding information** vs **creating things**. Here's how to know which you need.

## The core difference

- **Perplexity** = a research engine. Ask a question, get a synthesized answer **with cited sources** you can click and verify.
- **ChatGPT** = a general assistant. Write, code, brainstorm, generate images, analyze files — open-ended creation.

## Side-by-side

| | Perplexity | ChatGPT |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cited sources | Core strength | Available with browsing |
| Creation (writing/code) | Decent | Strong |
| Images / voice | Limited | Yes |
| Research depth | Strong | Good |
| Best for | Looking things up | Making things |

## When Perplexity wins

- You need answers you can **verify**
- You're doing **research** for work, study or fact-checking
- You want to **see the sources**, not just trust the model

## When ChatGPT wins

- You're **writing, coding or creating**
- You want **images, voice or file analysis**
- You need an **all-round** assistant for varied tasks

> **Our take:** this is the rare "use both" that's actually true. They barely overlap — Perplexity is your librarian, ChatGPT is your studio. Heavy users open Perplexity to research a topic, then switch to ChatGPT to turn the findings into something. Forcing a single winner means giving up half the value.

## How to decide

1. **Mostly looking things up?** Perplexity.
2. **Mostly making things?** ChatGPT.
3. **Both?** Use both — they complement, not compete.
