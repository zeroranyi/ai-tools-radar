AI video took a huge leap, and three names lead the conversation: Sora, Runway and Veo. They're not all the same kind of tool. Here's how to choose.

## The core distinction

- **Sora (OpenAI)** & **Veo (Google)** — **text-to-video generators**. Describe a scene, get a realistic clip. Generation-first.
- **Runway** — a **full creative tool**: generation *plus* editing, controls and a workflow built for creators.

## Side-by-side

| | Sora | Veo | Runway |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strength | Realistic generation | Realistic generation | Generation + editing |
| Control / editing | Limited | Limited | Extensive |
| Ecosystem | OpenAI | Google | Creative suite |
| Best for | Quick clips | Quick clips | Full creative work |

## Choose Sora or Veo if...

- You want **realistic clips from a prompt**, fast
- You're already in the **OpenAI** (Sora) or **Google** (Veo) ecosystem
- Generation quality matters more than editing control

## Choose Runway if...

- You need **editing, control and a creative workflow**
- You're a **filmmaker or marketer** refining shots
- You want more than one-shot generation

Generate with whichever model gives the best raw clip (often Sora or Veo), then bring it into a tool like Runway to edit, extend and control the final result. Treating it as "one tool to rule them all" misses that *generation* and *production* are different jobs. If you only want a quick clip, a generator is enough; if you're crafting something, you'll want Runway's controls.

## How to choose

1. **Quick realistic clips?** Sora or Veo.
2. **Full editing + control?** Runway.
3. **Serious creator?** Generate in one, refine in Runway.
