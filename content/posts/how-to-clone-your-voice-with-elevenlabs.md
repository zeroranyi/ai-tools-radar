Voice cloning sounds futuristic, but ElevenLabs makes it a few-minutes process. Here's how to do it well — and the consent rules you must not skip.

## Before you start: the consent rule

Only clone a voice you **own** or have **explicit permission** to use. Cloning someone else's voice without consent can be illegal and violates the terms of service. This isn't optional — it's the line between a useful tool and a serious problem.

## What you need

- An ElevenLabs account
- **Clean audio samples** of the voice (quiet room, consistent mic, no background noise)
- Text you want spoken

## Step-by-step

1. **Sign up** for ElevenLabs.
2. **Choose voice cloning** in the dashboard.
3. **Upload your samples** — clear, consistent recordings give the best result.
4. **Name and create** the voice.
5. **Type text** and generate speech in the cloned voice.
6. **Refine** — adjust settings (stability, clarity) to taste.

## Quality tips

- **Clean beats long** — a short clear sample outperforms lots of noisy audio
- Record in a **quiet room** with a consistent mic and distance
- Keep tone and pace **natural** in samples
- Test with varied text to check it holds up

## Good uses

Narration, audiobooks, content voiceover, dubbing, and accessibility (e.g. preserving your own voice). 

The technology is neutral; the use isn't. Clone your own voice freely, get written permission for anyone else's, never use a clone to deceive or impersonate, and disclose AI voice where it matters. The tools now ship with consent checks for a reason — respect them, and voice cloning is genuinely useful rather than a liability.

## Bottom line

Cloning your voice with ElevenLabs is quick: upload clean, consented audio and generate speech from text. Prioritize clean recordings for quality — and consent for everything else. Confirm current features and pricing on ElevenLabs' official site.
