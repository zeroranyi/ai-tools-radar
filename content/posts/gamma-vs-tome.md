Both Gamma and Tome turn a prompt into a presentation in seconds, but they suit different goals. Here's how to choose.

## The core difference

- **Gamma** — versatile and **editable**. Generates polished decks, documents and even webpages, easy to tweak. Great for everyday work.
- **Tome** — **narrative and design-forward**. Built for visually striking, story-driven presentations.

## Side-by-side

| | Gamma | Tome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Best for | Work decks, docs, fast output | Narrative, design-forward decks |
| Formats | Decks, docs, webpages | Presentations |
| Editing | Flexible, easy | Design-led |
| Polish | High | High (visual) |
| Versatility | Broad | Focused |

## Choose Gamma if...

- You need **fast, editable** decks for work
- You also want **docs or webpages** from the same tool
- Versatility and easy edits matter

## Choose Tome if...

- You're making a **pitch or showcase** where story and visuals lead
- You want **design-forward** output
- Narrative flow is the priority

Both get you to a *draft* fast, and both still need your editing to be presentation-ready — AI doesn't know your specific data, audience or argument. Gamma wins on "get a solid editable deck quickly for everyday work"; Tome wins on "make something visually memorable for a pitch." Judge by the deck you make most often.

## How to choose

1. **Everyday work decks (and docs)?** Gamma.
2. **Story-driven, visual pitches?** Tome.

Both have free tiers — generate one real deck in each. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
