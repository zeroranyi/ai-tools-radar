AI music tools fall into two camps: ones that write **complete songs** (vocals and all) and ones that make **background tracks** for video. Here are the best for each.

## Quick picks

| Goal | Best tool |
| --- | --- |
| Easy full songs with vocals | Suno |
| Audio quality & control | Udio |
| Royalty-free background music | Soundraw |

## Full songs: Suno vs Udio

For complete songs from a prompt, it comes down to **Suno** (easiest and fastest) versus **Udio** (finer audio quality and control). We break this down in [Suno vs Udio](/posts/suno-vs-udio) — most people start with Suno for speed and try Udio when they want more polish.

## Background music: Soundraw

If you need **royalty-free background tracks** for videos or podcasts rather than full songs, **Soundraw** lets you customize length and mood with commercial-safe licensing built in.

## The licensing thing creators learn the hard way

AI music is fun until you try to monetize a track and find the commercial terms don't cover your use. Before putting any AI music on YouTube, ads or streaming, confirm the **commercial-use rights** for your specific tier — this matters more than which tool sounds best in the demo.

## Bottom line

Suno for easy songs with vocals, Udio for quality and control, Soundraw for royalty-free background music. Test the vocals on your own prompt, and always verify commercial rights before you publish. Confirm current pricing on each official site, and browse more in the [AI Tools Directory](/tools).
