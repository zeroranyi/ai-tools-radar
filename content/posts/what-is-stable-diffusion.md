Stable Diffusion is the open-source backbone of much of the AI image world. Here's what it is and why people choose it over polished paid tools.

## What is Stable Diffusion?

**Stable Diffusion** is an **open-source** AI model that turns text prompts into images. "Open" is the key word: anyone can download and run it, build on it, and use it freely — which is why it powers countless apps and interfaces.

## How it works (simply)

It's a *diffusion* model: it learns to turn random noise into an image that matches your prompt, step by step. You don't need the math — what matters is the result: text in, image out, running on hardware you control.

## Why people choose it

- **Free** — no per-image fee, no watermark
- **Private** — when you run it locally, your prompts stay on your machine (hosted services vary)
- **Unlimited** — generate as much as your hardware allows
- **Controllable** — ControlNet, custom models, fine-tunes
- **Commercial-friendly** — broad usage (check the specific model license)

## The trade-off

| Stable Diffusion | Midjourney |
| --- | --- |
| Free, unlimited, private | Paid, hosted |
| Highly controllable | Easiest, most artistic |
| Setup + learning curve | Works instantly |

## How to use it

- **Easiest:** a free web interface
- **Most control:** run it locally (popular UIs make this approachable), similar in spirit to [running a local LLM](/posts/how-to-run-a-local-llm)
- **Compare options:** see [best free AI image generators](/posts/best-free-ai-image-generators)

## Bottom line

Stable Diffusion is the free, open, controllable foundation of AI imaging — unbeatable for unlimited, private generation if you'll invest a little setup. For instant polish with zero effort, a hosted tool like Midjourney is easier. It fits the broader [open-source AI](/posts/what-is-open-source-ai) trade-off: control and cost vs convenience.
