Descript and CapCut both use AI to speed up editing, but they're built for different content. Here's how to pick the right one.

## The core difference

- **Descript** — **text-based editing**. It transcribes your video, and you edit the video by editing the words. Magic for talking-head and podcast content.
- **CapCut** — **social video editor**. Fast captions, effects, templates and trends for short-form.

## Side-by-side

| | Descript | CapCut |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Best content | Podcasts, talking-head | Social, short-form |
| Editing style | Edit the transcript | Timeline + templates |
| Captions | Yes | Yes (fast/auto) |
| Effects & trends | Basic | Extensive |
| Repurposing | Strong | Good |

## Choose Descript if...

- You make **podcasts or talking-head** videos
- You want to **edit by editing text** and remove filler words easily
- You repurpose **spoken-word** content

## Choose CapCut if...

- You make **TikTok, Reels, Shorts**
- You want **fast captions, effects and templates**
- Visual flair and trends matter

Is your video mostly *someone talking* or mostly *visual/social*? Descript turns talking into an editable document — unbeatable for podcasts and tutorials. CapCut turns clips into punchy social videos — unbeatable for TikTok-style content. Pick by the *kind of video*, and the choice is obvious.

## How to choose

1. **Podcasts / talking-head / tutorials?** Descript.
2. **Social short-form with effects?** CapCut.

Both have free tiers — match to your content type. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
