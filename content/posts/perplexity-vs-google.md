Perplexity and Google answer the same impulse — "I want to know something" — in very different ways. Here's when each is the better tool.

## The fundamental difference

- **Perplexity** gives you a **direct, synthesized answer with citations**. You ask; it answers and links sources.
- **Google** gives you **a page of links and tools** (plus AI summaries). You explore and decide.

## When Perplexity wins

- You want a **straight answer**, not ten tabs
- You're **researching** a topic and want sources
- The question is **informational** ("how", "why", "compare")

## When Google wins

- **Navigation** ("open my bank's login")
- **Local** ("coffee near me"), **maps**, **shopping**
- You want to **browse many sources** yourself
- **Transactional** searches where you'll click through

## Side-by-side

| Need | Better tool |
| --- | --- |
| Direct cited answer | Perplexity |
| Research a topic | Perplexity |
| Navigation / login | Google |
| Local / maps / shopping | Google |
| Browse many sources | Google |

> **Our take:** the smart behavior emerging in 2026 is *both, by intent*. When you want to be **answered**, open Perplexity — it's faster and shows its sources. When you want to **browse, buy, or go somewhere**, Google still owns that. Treating it as "which one replaces the other" misses that they're optimized for different verbs.

## Bottom line

Use **Perplexity to get answered** and **Google to browse and transact**. They're not really rivals so much as two tools for two different kinds of search. Confirm Perplexity's current plans on its official site.
