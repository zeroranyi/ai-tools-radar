AI can genuinely help you learn faster — or get you in trouble, if you use it to cut corners. Here are the tools worth using, grouped by what you actually need them for.

## Quick picks by task

| Task | Best tool | Free? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Research with sources | Perplexity | Yes |
| Writing & explanations | Claude or ChatGPT | Yes |
| Lecture notes | Otter / Fireflies | Yes |
| Quick answers | Gemini | Yes |

## Research: Perplexity

For essays and assignments, Perplexity beats a plain chatbot because it **cites its sources**. You get an answer *and* the links to verify it — which is exactly what good academic work requires.

## Writing help: ChatGPT or Claude

Use these to **understand concepts, get feedback, and brainstorm structure** — not to write the essay for you. Claude tends to explain clearly and write naturally; ChatGPT is a strong all-rounder.

## Lecture notes: Otter or Fireflies

AI note-takers transcribe and summarize lectures so you can actually pay attention instead of scrambling to write. Great for revision later.

AI is a fantastic tutor and a terrible ghostwriter. Use it to learn, explain, and check — never to submit work you didn't produce. Most schools now detect and penalize AI-written submissions, and you learn nothing. Check your institution's policy before using AI on graded work.

## A simple, safe student stack

1. **Perplexity** to research with citations
2. **Claude or ChatGPT** to understand and draft (then rewrite in your own words)
3. **Otter** to capture lectures
4. **Grammarly** to polish

All of these have free tiers, so you can build this whole stack for $0 and upgrade only what you use most.
