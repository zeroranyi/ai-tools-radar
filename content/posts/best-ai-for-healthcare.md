Healthcare is drowning in documentation and admin — exactly where AI helps most. But it's also the highest-stakes field for privacy and accuracy. Here's how to use AI responsibly.

## The healthcare AI stack

| Task | Best AI | Hard rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Clinical notes | HIPAA-compliant AI scribes | Approved tools only |
| Admin & scheduling | Compliant assistants | No PHI in consumer AI |
| Patient-education drafts | ChatGPT / Claude | No patient data; verify |
| Research support | General assistants | Verify all clinical claims |

## Clinical documentation (the biggest win)

**AI scribes** built for healthcare can draft visit notes, cutting the documentation burden that drives burnout. Use only tools that are **compliant and approved** by your organization.

## Admin and patient education

General assistants speed up **non-clinical** admin and drafting **general patient-education** materials — as long as **no protected health information** is involved and clinical facts are verified.

## The two non-negotiables

> **Privacy:** never enter protected health information (PHI) into general consumer AI like a standard ChatGPT account. Use only HIPAA-compliant, organization-approved tools for anything involving patient data. A privacy breach here has legal and ethical weight far beyond an inconvenience.
>
> **Accuracy:** AI can be confidently wrong, and in medicine that's dangerous. AI supports documentation and information-gathering — it does not replace clinical judgment. Verify every clinical claim.

These aren't suggestions; in healthcare they're the boundary between a helpful tool and serious harm.

## How to start

1. Adopt a **compliant AI scribe** for documentation (with approval).
2. Use general assistants only for **non-PHI admin and drafting**.
3. **Verify** all clinical content and follow your institution's policy.

Confirm compliance, security and pricing on each official site before use.
