AI detectors estimate whether text was written by AI. They're used by teachers, editors and publishers — but they're probabilistic tools, not lie detectors. Here are the best ones and how to read their results honestly.

## The best AI detectors by use case

| Detector | Best for | Free tier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Originality.ai** | Publishers, SEO teams, agencies | Limited |
| **GPTZero** | Educators, general use | Yes |
| **Copyleaks** | AI + plagiarism together | Limited |
| **Turnitin** | Academic institutions | Via institution |

## Originality.ai — best for publishers

Built for content teams, it combines **AI detection and plagiarism** scanning with a team workflow and API. The go-to when you're checking freelancer or agency content at scale.

## GPTZero — best for educators and general use

The most accessible detector, with a usable free tier. Popular in education for a quick read on student submissions — but see the accuracy caveat below before acting on a score.

## Copyleaks — AI plus plagiarism

Strong when you need **both** AI-detection and traditional plagiarism checking in one scan, common in academic and enterprise settings.

## Turnitin — inside academia

Most students encounter AI detection through Turnitin, which is integrated into university submission systems rather than bought individually.

## The accuracy caveat you must understand

No detector is reliably accurate. They output a **probability**, and they get it wrong in both directions — flagging genuine human writing (false positives) and missing edited AI text (false negatives). That's why a detector score should be a **signal that prompts a conversation**, never sole proof to accuse someone. If you write, you may also want to understand [whether AI content is detectable at all](/posts/is-ai-content-detectable) and [how people humanize AI text](/posts/how-to-humanize-ai-text).

## Bottom line

Pick Originality.ai for publishing, GPTZero for education and general use, Copyleaks for combined checks, and expect Turnitin in academia. Whichever you choose, read scores as probabilities — corroborate, don't accuse. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
