When you ask Perplexity or ChatGPT Search a question and get a written answer with sources, something different from classic search is happening. Here's how it works.

## The two-step engine: retrieve + generate

AI search combines two things:

1. **Retrieval** — your question triggers a search of the web (or a set of sources) for the most relevant pages.
2. **Generation** — a language model **reads those sources** and writes a synthesized answer, usually **with citations**.

So instead of a list of links, you get an **answer grounded in freshly retrieved sources**. This is RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) applied to the open web.

## Step by step

1. You ask a question.
2. The system **interprets intent** and forms search queries.
3. It **retrieves** relevant pages.
4. The model **reads and synthesizes** them into an answer.
5. It **cites** the sources it used.

## Why citations matter

Because the answer is *generated*, it can still be wrong even with good sources. Reputable AI search **shows its sources** so you can verify — which is exactly why you should click them for anything important.

## Classic search vs AI search

| | Classic search | AI search |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Output | List of links | Synthesized answer |
| Sources | You explore | Retrieved & cited |
| Effort | You read & decide | Answer up front |
| Risk | Wrong link | Wrong synthesis (verify) |

> **What this means if you publish online:** getting *cited* in AI answers is the new version of ranking. AI engines pull from content they can confidently extract — clear direct answers, data, clean structure, trustworthy sourcing. Optimizing for that is GEO (generative engine optimization). Understanding that AI search *retrieves then writes* explains both why citations exist and why structured, sourced content gets surfaced.

## Bottom line

AI search retrieves relevant sources, then a language model synthesizes a cited answer from them. It hands you an answer instead of links — fast and convenient, but always worth verifying via the citations it provides.
