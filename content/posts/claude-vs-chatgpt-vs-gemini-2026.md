The three leading general-purpose AI assistants in 2026 are **ChatGPT (OpenAI)**, **Claude (Anthropic)**, and **Gemini (Google)**. They overlap heavily, but each has a clear sweet spot. This guide compares them on the things that actually matter, then gives a recommendation per use case.

## Quick comparison

| Criterion | ChatGPT | Claude | Gemini |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Best for | All-round use | Writing & coding | Google ecosystem |
| Free tier | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Paid price | ~$20/mo | ~$20/mo | ~$20/mo |
| Context window | Large | Very large | Very large |
| Image generation | Yes | No | Yes |
| Voice mode | Yes | Limited | Yes |
| Workspace integration | Limited | Limited | Native (Gmail/Docs) |

## Which should you pick?

- **Choose ChatGPT** if you want one tool that does a bit of everything — text, images, voice, and a huge ecosystem of apps.
- **Choose Claude** if your work is mostly writing or coding. Its prose reads more naturally and it handles large documents and codebases gracefully.
- **Choose Gemini** if you live inside Gmail, Docs and Sheets — the integration saves real time.

## Writing quality

For long-form writing, Claude generally produces the most natural, least "AI-sounding" prose. ChatGPT is a close second and more flexible with formats. Gemini is competitive and especially handy when drafting inside Google Docs.

## Coding

Claude and ChatGPT lead here. Claude's long context makes it strong for refactoring and understanding large codebases; ChatGPT pairs well with IDE extensions and has broad language coverage.

## Price and value

All three cost roughly $20/month for their paid tiers, and all three have genuinely useful free tiers. Start free, then upgrade the one that matches your main use case.

> Bottom line: there's no single winner. Match the tool to the job — ChatGPT for versatility, Claude for writing and code, Gemini for Google-centric workflows.
