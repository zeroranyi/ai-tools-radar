Notion AI and ChatGPT get compared constantly, but they are built for different jobs. One is an AI layer inside a workspace; the other is a general-purpose assistant. Knowing the difference saves you from paying for the wrong tool.

## The short version

- **Notion AI** is an AI layer baked into Notion. Its superpower is *context* — it reads and writes your existing pages, summarizes meeting notes, and fills in database properties without you leaving the app.
- **ChatGPT** is a standalone assistant. Its superpower is *capability* — selectable models, web browsing, file analysis, image generation, voice and coding.

## Side-by-side comparison

| Criterion | Notion AI | ChatGPT |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Works on your own docs/data | Yes (native) | No (manual paste) |
| Choose the model | No | Yes |
| Web access | Limited | Yes |
| Image generation | No | Yes |
| Coding | Basic | Strong |
| Team docs & collaboration | Native | Limited |
| Pricing | Bundled into Notion paid plans | Free tier + paid (~$20/mo) |

> Pricing for both changes regularly. Check the official Notion and OpenAI pricing pages before you buy.

## When to choose Notion AI

Pick Notion AI if your team already runs on Notion. Because it acts *inside* your workspace, it removes the copy-paste loop:

- Summarize a long meeting note in place
- Ask questions across your own knowledge base
- Auto-fill database fields (status, tags, summaries)
- Draft and rewrite directly in the doc you're editing

## When to choose ChatGPT

Pick ChatGPT when the work is open-ended or technical:

- Drafting content from scratch
- Coding and debugging
- Research with web access
- Generating images or analyzing files

## The honest answer: use both

These tools aren't mutually exclusive, and treating the choice as either/or is the most common mistake. A practical setup:

1. **Create** with ChatGPT — first drafts, code, research.
2. **Organize and retrieve** with Notion AI — store it, summarize it, query it later.

If you can only pay for one and you don't already live in Notion, start with ChatGPT for its broader capability. If your whole workflow is already in Notion, the built-in AI pays for itself in saved context-switching.
