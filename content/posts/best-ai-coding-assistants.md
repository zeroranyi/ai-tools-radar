There are three different *types* of AI coding tool, and picking the wrong type is why people get disappointed. Here's how to match the tool to how you actually work.

## The three types

| Type | Example | Best when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AI-first editor | Cursor | You want project-wide, agentic edits |
| Editor plugin | GitHub Copilot | You want autocomplete in your current editor |
| Chat assistant | Claude / ChatGPT | You want to reason through hard problems |

## Cursor — the AI-first editor

Cursor replaces your editor and understands your **whole codebase**, making it the strongest for multi-file edits and refactors. Best for developers working in real projects daily.

## GitHub Copilot — the plugin

Copilot stays inside the editor you already use and provides fast **inline autocomplete**. Lowest-friction option, great if you don't want to change your setup.

## Claude — the reasoning assistant

For genuinely hard problems, debugging, or planning a big refactor, a chat assistant like Claude (or ChatGPT) lets you **think out loud** with an AI that has long context. Many developers pair this with Cursor or Copilot.

> **Our take:** these aren't strictly competitors — the best setup is often *two* of them: an in-editor tool (Cursor or Copilot) for day-to-day coding, plus a chat assistant (Claude) for the gnarly problems. Don't expect one tool to do both jobs perfectly.

## How to choose

1. **Want AI woven through your whole project?** Cursor.
2. **Love your current editor and just want autocomplete?** Copilot.
3. **Need to reason through hard problems?** Claude (alongside one of the above).

All three have free ways to start. Try them on a real task — autocomplete quality and codebase understanding are best judged on your own code. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
