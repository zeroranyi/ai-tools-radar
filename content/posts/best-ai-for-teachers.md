AI can give teachers back hours of prep time each week — if used thoughtfully. Here are the tools worth using, grouped by task, with honest guidance on where to keep the human in charge.

## The teacher stack by task

| Task | Tools | Free? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lesson planning | ChatGPT / Claude / MagicSchool | Yes |
| Level-adapted reading | Diffit | Yes |
| Interactive lessons & quizzes | Curipod | Varies |
| Student tutoring | Khanmigo | Varies |
| Feedback drafts | Claude / ChatGPT | Yes |

## Lesson planning and materials

A general assistant can draft a **lesson plan, worksheet, rubric or activity** in minutes — then you adapt it to your class. **MagicSchool** packages 60+ teacher tasks into one purpose-built toolkit, which many educators prefer over a blank chatbot prompt.

## Differentiation and quizzes

**Diffit** is the standout for adapting a text to different reading levels; **Curipod** turns a topic into an interactive lesson with quizzes; or ask a general assistant to generate **variations** for different learners.

## Feedback and tutoring

AI can draft **feedback starters** and keep comments consistent — then you add your judgment. **Khanmigo** offers guided student tutoring with education-appropriate guardrails.

AI is a brilliant *prep assistant* and a poor *final authority*. Always check materials for accuracy (it can be confidently wrong), keep grading judgment with you (especially for subjective or high-stakes work), and follow your school's AI policy. Used this way, AI frees you to spend less time on paperwork and more on actual teaching — which is the whole point.

## How to start

1. Use **ChatGPT/Claude or MagicSchool** (free tiers) for plans, materials and feedback.
2. Add **Diffit/Curipod** for differentiation and interactive lessons.
3. Always **review and adapt** — you're the expert on your students.
