ChatGPT won't rank pages for you, but it can remove hours of grunt work from your SEO process. Here is a workflow that speeds things up **without** tripping Google's spam filters.

## Step 1: Cluster keywords by intent

Paste a raw keyword list and ask ChatGPT to group terms into topic clusters and label each by search intent (informational, commercial, navigational). This reveals which pages to build and how to interlink them.

> Tip: ChatGPT can't see real search volume. Get that from a keyword tool, then use ChatGPT to organize and prioritize.

## Step 2: Build a content brief

Ask for an outline based on a target keyword and the intent. A good brief includes the H2/H3 structure, questions to answer, and entities to mention.

## Step 3: Draft — then add what AI can't

Use ChatGPT for a first draft, then add the things that make content rank and get cited:

- Original data, screenshots or first-hand testing
- A clear point of view and recommendation
- Accurate, sourced facts (verify every claim)

## Step 4: Optimize on-page

Have ChatGPT generate meta titles, descriptions, an answer-first intro, and FAQ entries you can mark up with FAQ schema.

## Step 5: Review before publishing

Fact-check, run a plagiarism/AI check, and make sure each page genuinely helps the reader. This single step is what separates content that ranks from content that gets de-indexed.

> The rule: ChatGPT accelerates the work; a human guarantees the quality.
