Small businesses don't need a dozen AI subscriptions — they need a few tools that save real time. Here's a lean, practical stack grouped by what actually moves the needle.

## The starter stack (most businesses)

| Need | Tool | Free tier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General work & content | ChatGPT or Claude | Yes |
| Meeting notes | Otter | Yes |
| Marketing copy at volume | Jasper or Copy.ai | Limited |
| Customer support | An AI chatbot | Varies |

## 1. One general assistant (start here)

A single assistant — ChatGPT or Claude — covers an enormous range: drafting emails, writing product descriptions, summarizing documents, answering customer questions, brainstorming. For many small businesses, **this one tool is 80% of the value.**

## 2. Meeting notes: Otter

If you take calls with clients or your team, an AI note-taker captures and summarizes them automatically. Cheap time-saver.

## 3. Marketing: Jasper or Copy.ai (only if you publish a lot)

If you produce regular marketing content, these add templates and brand voice. If you post occasionally, your general assistant is enough — don't pay twice.

## 4. Customer support: an AI chatbot

AI can handle FAQs and triage, freeing you for the conversations that need a human. Always keep an easy path to a real person.

Subscribing to five AI tools because each looks useful, then paying for features you never touch. The disciplined move is to run *one general assistant* for a month, notice where it genuinely falls short, and add a specialized tool only for that specific gap. Most owners never need more than two or three.

## How to build your stack

1. Start with **one general assistant's free tier**.
2. Add a **note-taker** if you're in lots of meetings.
3. Add **marketing or support tools** only when volume justifies them.

Lean and intentional beats a pile of subscriptions. Confirm current pricing on each official site before paying.
