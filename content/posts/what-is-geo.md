## What is GEO?

**Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** is the practice of creating and structuring content so that AI search engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude and others — cite it, quote it, and fold it into the answers they generate. The term comes from a 2023 research paper that studied what makes AI engines pick one source over another.

Traditional SEO chases a ranking and a click. GEO chases something different: **being the source the AI trusts enough to repeat.**

## GEO vs SEO

| | SEO | GEO |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Goal | Rank high, earn the click | Get cited in the AI answer |
| Success metric | Position, organic traffic | Citations, AI share of voice |
| User behavior | Clicks the blue link | May never click (zero-click) |
| Favors | Authority + relevance | Clear, sourced, extractable facts |

The two overlap on fundamentals — technical health, trustworthy content, and structured data all help both. The difference is the end goal.

## How generative engines pick sources

AI engines synthesize answers from content they can confidently extract and attribute. The original GEO research found that adding **citations, statistics and authoritative quotations** measurably increased how often content was included in AI answers — while keyword stuffing did almost nothing.

In other words: AI engines reward content that *reads like a reliable source*, not content that games keywords.

## How to optimize for GEO

1. **Answer first.** Open with a concise, direct answer to the question in the title.
2. **Cite and quantify.** Include real statistics, sources and quotes — the strongest signals from the research.
3. **Use structured formats.** Tables, numbered steps and FAQs are easy for AI to extract.
4. **Add FAQ and Article structured data** so machines parse your content cleanly.
5. **Keep paragraphs short** and facts clearly stated.
6. **Stay accurate.** A single wrong fact undermines the trust GEO depends on.

## GEO's limits

GEO is new and its metrics aren't yet standardized. You can't fully control how any model uses your content, and best practices will shift as engines evolve. Treat GEO as a layer on top of solid SEO — not a replacement for it.

> Bottom line: SEO gets you ranked; GEO gets you quoted. In an AI-search world, you want both.
