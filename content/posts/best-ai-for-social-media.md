AI can keep your social media consistent without consuming your whole week — but only if you keep it sounding like you. Here's a practical stack by content type.

## The social stack

| Need | Best AI | Free tier |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Captions, hooks, ideas | ChatGPT / Claude | Yes |
| Graphics & carousels | Canva AI | Yes |
| Short-form video | Opus Clip / CapCut | Limited |
| Scheduling & planning | AI in schedulers | Varies |

## Captions and ideas

A general assistant generates **captions, hooks and content ideas** at volume — beating the blank page. Feed it your topic and audience, then edit for voice.

## Graphics

**Canva AI** makes social graphics, carousels and posts fast, even without design skills.

## Short-form video

**Opus Clip** turns long videos into clips automatically; **CapCut** adds captions and effects. Short-form is the biggest growth lever, so this is high-value.

## Planning

AI features in **schedulers** suggest times, draft captions and help you batch — so you plan once and publish all week.

Posting generic, obviously-AI captions. Audiences scroll past bland, and algorithms favor content that earns engagement — which generic posts don't. Use AI to *beat the blank page and produce volume*, then add your specific take, story or hook. The winning workflow is AI for the first draft and quantity, you for the authentic voice that actually connects. Volume without voice is just noise.

## How to start

1. **ChatGPT/Claude** for captions and ideas.
2. **Canva AI** for graphics.
3. **Opus Clip/CapCut** if video is your focus.

Most have free tiers — build lean. Confirm current pricing on each official site.
