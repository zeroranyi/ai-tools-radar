When ChatGPT stops working, the fix depends on whether the problem is OpenAI's or yours. Here's how to tell, and the fixes that resolve most cases.

## Step 1: Is it down for everyone, or just you?

Before troubleshooting, check whether it's an outage:

- **status.openai.com** — OpenAI's official status page
- **Downdetector** — spikes mean a widespread problem

If it's a confirmed outage, no local fix helps — wait it out, or switch to an [alternative like Claude or Gemini](/posts/chatgpt-alternatives) to keep working.

## The 9 most common fixes

| # | Problem | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Page frozen / not loading | Refresh; start a new chat |
| 2 | Stuck response | Stop and resend the message |
| 3 | Login won't work | Log out/in; check Google/Microsoft sign-in |
| 4 | "Too many requests" | Rate limit — wait, slow down, or upgrade |
| 5 | Network error | Switch Wi-Fi/data; check VPN |
| 6 | Weird display/glitches | Clear cache or use incognito |
| 7 | Features missing | Disable conflicting browser extensions |
| 8 | Won't load at all | Try a different browser or device |
| 9 | Still broken | It may be an outage — check status |

## "Too many requests" explained

This is a **rate limit**, not a fault with your account — common on the free tier or during peak load. Wait, send messages less rapidly, or move to a paid plan for higher limits (see [is ChatGPT Plus worth it](/posts/is-chatgpt-plus-worth-it)).

## When it's an outage, switch

If status pages confirm ChatGPT is down, the fastest "fix" is to keep working elsewhere: Claude, Gemini, Perplexity or DeepSeek can all stand in for most tasks. Our [ChatGPT alternatives guide](/posts/chatgpt-alternatives) covers which to use for what.

## Bottom line

Start by checking whether ChatGPT is down globally; if not, the fixes above resolve most issues — refresh, re-login, clear cache, switch browser/network, and respect rate limits. During real outages, a backup assistant keeps you productive.
