Cursor's pricing is a familiar three-tier setup. Here's what each gives you and who it's for, with current figures. *Prices are as of 2026 — confirm on Cursor's official pricing page.*

## The plans at a glance

| Plan | Price (as of 2026) | For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hobby** | Free | Trying it out, light use |
| **Pro** | ~$20/mo | Individual developers, full access |
| **Business** | ~$40/user/mo | Teams, admin & privacy controls |

## Free tier

Limited AI requests and model access — enough to **evaluate** whether Cursor fits your workflow. Heavy use hits limits fast.

## Pro (~$20/mo)

The plan most individual developers want: **full access to the best models**, higher limits, and the agentic, whole-codebase editing Cursor is known for.

## Business (~$40/user/mo)

Adds **team features** — centralized billing, admin controls, and privacy/security options. Priced per seat, so it scales with team size.

Cursor Pro sits at the same ~$20 as a ChatGPT or Claude subscription, but it's a *coding-specific* spend. For a developer shipping real code daily, the multi-file agentic editing can save hours a week — easily worth it. If you just want autocomplete, GitHub Copilot at ~$10 is half the price. So the pricing question is really "do I want a full AI editor (Cursor Pro, ~$20) or just autocomplete (Copilot, ~$10)?"

## Bottom line

Cursor is free to try (Hobby), ~$20/mo for individuals (Pro), and ~$40/user/mo for teams (Business). Most serious developers want Pro; compare against Copilot's ~$10 if you only need autocomplete. Confirm current pricing on Cursor's official site.
