Cursor's pricing is a familiar three-tier setup. Here's what each gives you and who it's for. *Confirm live numbers on Cursor's official pricing page.*

## The plans at a glance

| Plan | Roughly | For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Free** | $0 | Trying it out, light use |
| **Pro** | ~$20/mo | Individual developers, full access |
| **Business** | Per user | Teams, admin & privacy controls |

## Free tier

Limited AI requests and model access — enough to **evaluate** whether Cursor fits your workflow. Heavy use hits limits fast.

## Pro (~$20/mo)

The plan most individual developers want: **full access to the best models**, higher limits, and the agentic, whole-codebase editing Cursor is known for.

## Business (per user)

Adds **team features** — centralized billing, admin controls, and privacy/security options. Priced per seat, so it scales with team size.

> **The value framing:** Cursor Pro sits at the same ~$20 as a ChatGPT or Claude subscription, but it's a *coding-specific* spend. If you're a developer shipping real code daily, the multi-file agentic editing can save hours a week — easily worth it. If you just want autocomplete, GitHub Copilot at ~$10 is cheaper. So the pricing question is really "do I want a full AI editor (Cursor Pro) or just autocomplete (Copilot)?"

## Bottom line

Cursor is **free to try, ~$20/mo for individuals (Pro), per-user for teams (Business)**. Most serious developers want Pro; compare against Copilot if you only need autocomplete. Confirm current pricing on Cursor's official site.
