Most AI image generators turn text into gibberish. Ideogram's claim to fame is that it doesn't. This review covers what it's best at and where it isn't the right tool.

## What Ideogram is best at

**Readable text inside images.** Logos, posters, quote cards, ad creative, signage — anything where the words have to come out right. This is the single most common failure of other generators, and Ideogram's main edge.

## How it compares

| Need | Best tool |
| --- | --- |
| Text in images | Ideogram |
| Artistic quality | Midjourney |
| Easy, conversational | ChatGPT's generator |
| Free & controllable | Stable Diffusion |

For the broader field, see [best AI image generators](/posts/best-ai-image-generators) and [Midjourney alternatives](/posts/midjourney-alternatives).

## Where it isn't the pick

For purely artistic, text-free images, [Midjourney](/posts/is-midjourney-worth-it) generally produces more striking results, and ChatGPT's built-in generator is easier for casual use. Ideogram is a specialist — reach for it when text accuracy matters.

## Pricing

Free tier with limited generations; paid plans for more volume. Confirm current pricing on Ideogram's official site.

## Bottom line

Ideogram is the best AI image generator when your image needs **accurate, readable text** — a genuine gap it fills better than Midjourney or DALL·E. For text-free art, look elsewhere. Try the free tier to test its text rendering, and confirm current pricing on its official site.
