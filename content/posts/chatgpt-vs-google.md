ChatGPT and Google answer the same urge — "I want to know something" — in opposite ways. One *makes and explains*; the other *finds and links*. Here's when to use which.

## The core difference

- **ChatGPT** — an assistant that **creates, explains and synthesizes**. Ask it to write, code, summarize or walk you through something.
- **Google** — a search engine that **connects you to the live web**: many sources, navigation, local, shopping, real-time.

## When to use ChatGPT

- Writing, coding, brainstorming
- Explaining a concept in plain language
- Summarizing or transforming text you provide
- Step-by-step help on a task

## When to use Google

| Need | Why Google |
| --- | --- |
| Navigation ("open my bank") | Direct to the site |
| Local ("dentist near me") | Maps, hours, reviews |
| Shopping / prices | Live listings |
| Breaking news | Real-time, multiple sources |
| Verify a fact | Compare many sources yourself |

## They're converging — but the rule holds

ChatGPT can now browse the web, and Google shows AI answers (AI Overviews). The lines blur, but the intent split stays useful: **ChatGPT to make and understand, Google to find and verify**. And because ChatGPT can [hallucinate](/posts/what-is-ai-hallucination), the web still matters for important facts. If you want AI answers *with sources*, [Perplexity](/posts/perplexity-vs-google) sits neatly between the two.

## Bottom line

Don't pick one — use both by intent. Open ChatGPT to create, explain or synthesize; use Google for the live web, local, shopping and verification. For a wider view of assistants, see [best AI chatbots](/posts/best-ai-chatbots) or browse the [AI Tools Directory](/tools).
